Theorem 4.1.1.

Suppose that \(x_1(t)\) and \(x_2(t)\) are two eigenfunctions of the problem (4.1), (4.2), or (4.3) for two different eigenvalues \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\text{.}\) Then they are orthogonal in the sense that
\begin{equation} \int_a^b x_1(t) x_2(t) \,dt = 0 . \end{equation}
