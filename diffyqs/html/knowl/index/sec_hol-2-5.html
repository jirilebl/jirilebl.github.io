Theorem 2.3.1. Superposition.

If \(y_1\text{,}\) \(y_2\text{,}\) ..., \(y_n\) are solutions of the homogeneous equation (2.4), then
\begin{equation} y(x) = C_1 y_1(x) + C_2 y_2(x) + \cdots + C_n y_n(x) \end{equation}
also solves (2.4) for arbitrary constants \(C_1, C_2, \ldots, C_n\text{.}\)
