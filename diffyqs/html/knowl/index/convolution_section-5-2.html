\begin{equation} x(t) = f(t) + \int_0^t g(t-\tau) x(\tau) \, d\tau . \end{equation}
Here \(f(t)\) and \(g(t)\) are known functions and \(x(t)\) is an unknown we wish to solve for. We have \(x(t) = f(t) + (g * x)(t)\text{,}\) so the question is if convolution is like a product, can we also ‚Äúdivide‚Äù to solve this equation. We apply the Laplace transform to the equation to obtain
\begin{equation} X(s) = F(s) + G(s) X(s) , \end{equation}
where \(X(s)\text{,}\) \(F(s)\text{,}\) and \(G(s)\) are the Laplace transforms of \(x(t)\text{,}\) \(f(t)\text{,}\) and \(g(t)\) respectively. We find

To find \(x(t)\text{,}\) we now need to find the inverse Laplace transform of \(X(s)\text{.}\)
