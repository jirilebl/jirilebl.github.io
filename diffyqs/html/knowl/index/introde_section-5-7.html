Normally, the \(k\) in \(\frac{dP}{dt} = kP\) is given, and we want to solve the equation for different initial conditions. What does that mean? Take \(k=1\) for simplicity: We want to solve the equation \(\frac{dP}{dt} = P\) subject to \(P(0) = 1000\) (the initial condition). Then the solution is (exercise)
\begin{equation*} P(t) = 1000 \, e^t . \end{equation*}
We call \(P(t) = C e^t\) the general solution, as every solution of the equation can be written in this form for some constant \(C\text{.}\) We need an initial condition to find out what \(C\) is, in order to find the particular solution we are looking for.
