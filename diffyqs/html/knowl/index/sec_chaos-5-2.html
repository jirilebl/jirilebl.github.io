Draw the three vertices of a triangle and label them, say \(p_1\text{,}\) \(p_2\) and \(p_3\text{.}\) Draw some random point \(p\) (it does not have to be one of the three points above). Roll a die to pick of the \(p_1\text{,}\) \(p_2\text{,}\) or \(p_3\) randomly (for example 1 and 4 mean \(p_1\text{,}\) 2 and 5 mean \(p_2\text{,}\) and 3 and 6 mean \(p_3\)). Suppose we picked \(p_2\text{,}\) then let \(p_{\text{new}}\) be the point exactly halfway between \(p\) and \(p_2\text{.}\) Draw this point and let \(p\) now refer to this new point \(p_{\text{new}}\text{.}\) Rinse, repeat. Try to be precise and draw as many iterations as possible. Your points will be attracted to the so-called Sierpinski triangle.
