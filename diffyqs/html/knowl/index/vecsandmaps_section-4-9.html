The reason these are called a basis is that every other vector can be written as a unique linear combination of them. For example, in \({\mathbb R}^3\) the vector \((4,5,6)\) can be written as
\begin{equation} 4 \vec{e}_1 + 5 \vec{e}_2 + 6 \vec{e}_3 = 4 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + 5 \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} + 6 \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 5 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation}
