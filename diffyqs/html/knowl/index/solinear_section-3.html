Consider the general second-order linear differential equation
\begin{equation} A(x) y'' + B(x)y' + C(x)y = F(x) . \end{equation}
We often divide through by \(A(x)\) to get
\begin{equation} y'' + p(x)y' + q(x)y = f(x) ,\tag{2.1} \end{equation}
where \(p(x) = \nicefrac{B(x)}{A(x)}\text{,}\) \(q(x) = \nicefrac{C(x)}{A(x)}\text{,}\) and \(f(x) = \nicefrac{F(x)}{A(x)}\text{.}\) The word linear means that the equation contains no powers or functions of \(y\text{,}\) \(y'\text{,}\) and \(y''\text{.}\)
