Complex numbers may seem a strange concept, perhaps due to the terminology. There is nothing imaginary or complicated about complex numbers. A complex number is simply a pair of real numbers, \((a,b)\text{,}\) that is, it is a point in the plane. We add complex numbers in the straightforward way: \((a,b)+(c,d)=(a+c,b+d)\text{.}\) We define multiplication by
\begin{equation} (a,b) \times (c,d) \overset{\text{def}}{=} (ac-bd,ad+bc) . \end{equation}
It turns out that with this multiplication rule, all the standard properties of arithmetic hold. Further, and most importantly \((0,1) \times (0,1) = (-1,0)\text{.}\)
