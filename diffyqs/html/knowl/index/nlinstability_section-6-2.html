An equation of the form
\begin{equation} x'' + f(x) = 0, \end{equation}
where \(f(x)\) is an arbitrary function, is called a conservative equation. For example, the pendulum equation is a conservative equation. The equations are conservative as there is no friction in the system so the energy in the system is ‚Äúconserved.‚Äù Let us write this equation as a system of nonlinear ODEs.
\begin{equation} x' = y, \qquad y' = -f(x) . \end{equation}
These types of equations have the advantage that we can solve for their trajectories easily.
