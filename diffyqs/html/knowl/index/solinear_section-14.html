Theorem 2.1.2. Existence and uniqueness.

Suppose \(p, q, f\) are continuous functions on some interval \(I\text{,}\) \(a\) is a number in \(I\text{,}\) and \(b_0, b_1\) are constants. Then the equation
\begin{equation} y'' + p(x) y' + q(x) y = f(x) , \end{equation}
has exactly one solution \(y(x)\) defined on the interval \(I\) satisfying the initial conditions
\begin{equation} y(a) = b_0 , \qquad y'(a) = b_1 . \end{equation}
