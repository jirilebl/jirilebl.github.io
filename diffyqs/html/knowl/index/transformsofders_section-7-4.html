Example 6.2.5.

An equation containing an integral of the unknown function is called an integral equation. Consider
\begin{equation} x(t) - t = \int_0^t x(\tau) \, d\tau , \end{equation}
where we wish to solve for \(x(t)\text{.}\) We apply the Laplace transform to get
\begin{equation} X(s) - \frac{1}{s^2} = \frac{1}{s} X(s) , \end{equation}
where \(X(s) = \mathcal{L} \bigl\{ x(t) \bigr\}\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation} X(s) = \frac{1}{s(s-1)} = \frac{1}{s-1} - \frac{1}{s} . \end{equation}
The inverse Laplace transform gives
\begin{equation} x(t) = e^t - 1 . \end{equation}
