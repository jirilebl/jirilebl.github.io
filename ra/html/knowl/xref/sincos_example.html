Example 3.2.6.

The functions \(\sin(x)\) and \(\cos(x)\) are continuous. In the following computations we use the sum-to-product trigonometric identities. We also use the simple facts that \(\babs{\sin(x)} \leq \sabs{x}\text{,}\) \(\babs{\cos(x)} \leq 1\text{,}\) and \(\babs{\sin(x)} \leq 1\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \begin{split} \babs{\sin(x)-\sin(c)} & = \abs{ 2 \sin \left( \frac{x-c}{2} \right) \cos \left( \frac{x+c}{2} \right) } \\ & = 2 \abs{ \sin \left( \frac{x-c}{2} \right) } \abs{ \cos \left( \frac{x+c}{2} \right) } \\ & \leq 2 \abs{ \sin \left( \frac{x-c}{2} \right) } \\ & \leq 2 \abs{ \frac{x-c}{2} } = \sabs{x-c} \end{split} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \begin{split} \babs{\cos(x)-\cos(c)} & = \abs{ -2 \sin \left( \frac{x-c}{2} \right) \sin \left( \frac{x+c}{2} \right) } \\ & = 2 \abs{ \sin \left( \frac{x-c}{2} \right) } \abs{ \sin \left( \frac{x+c}{2} \right) } \\ & \leq 2 \abs{ \sin \left( \frac{x-c}{2} \right) } \\ & \leq 2 \abs{ \frac{x-c}{2} } = \sabs{x-c} \end{split} \end{equation}
🔗
The claim that \(\sin\) and \(\cos\) are continuous follows by taking an arbitrary sequence \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converging to \(c\text{,}\) or by applying the definition of continuity directly. Details are left to the reader.
