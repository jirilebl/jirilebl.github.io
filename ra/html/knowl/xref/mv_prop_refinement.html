Proposition 10.1.5.

Suppose \(R \subset \R^n\) is a closed rectangle, \(P\) is a partition of \(R\text{,}\) and \(\widetilde{P}\) is a refinement of \(P\text{.}\) If \(f \colon R \to \R\) is bounded, then
\begin{equation} L(P,f) \leq L(\widetilde{P},f) \qquad \text{and} \qquad U(\widetilde{P},f) \leq U(P,f) . \end{equation}
