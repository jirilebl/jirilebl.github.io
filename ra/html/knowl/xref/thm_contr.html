Theorem 7.6.2. Contraction mapping principle or Banach fixed point theorem.

 1 
Named after the Polish mathematician Stefan Banach (1892–1945) who first stated the theorem in 1922.
Let \((X,d)\) be a nonempty complete metric space and \(\varphi \colon X \to X\) a contraction. Then \(\varphi\) has a unique fixed point.
