Exercise 5.2.18.

Prove a version of the Riemann–Lebesgue Lemma (one of several so named): Suppose \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) is continuous and define the sequence \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) by
\begin{equation} x_n \coloneqq \int_a^b f(t) \sin(nt) \, dt . \end{equation}
Prove that \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} x_n = 0\text{.}\)
