Proposition 7.3.6.

Let \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) be a sequence in a metric space \((X,d)\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. If \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges to \(p \in X\text{,}\) then every subsequence \(\{ x_{n_k} \}_{k=1}^\infty\) converges to \(p\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If for some \(K \in \N\) the \(K\)-tail \(\{ x_n \}_{n=K+1}^\infty\) converges to \(p \in X\text{,}\) then \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges to \(p\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
