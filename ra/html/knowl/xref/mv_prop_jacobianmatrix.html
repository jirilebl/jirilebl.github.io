Proposition 8.3.9.

Let \(U \subset \R^n\) be open and let \(f \colon U \to \R^m\) be differentiable at \(p \in U\text{.}\) Then all the partial derivatives at \(p\) exist and, in terms of the standard bases of \(\R^n\) and \(\R^m\text{,}\) \(f'(p)\) is represented by the matrix
\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1}(p) & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_2}(p) & \ldots & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_n}(p) \\[6pt] \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_1}(p) & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_2}(p) & \ldots & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_n}(p) \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_1}(p) & \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_2}(p) & \ldots & \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_n}(p) \end{bmatrix} . \end{equation}
