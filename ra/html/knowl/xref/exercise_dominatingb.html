Exercise 1.1.9.

Let \(S\) be an ordered set and let \(A\) be a nonempty subset such that \(\sup \, A\) exists. Suppose there is a \(B \subset A\) such that whenever \(x \in A\) there is a \(y \in B\) such that \(x \leq y\text{.}\) Show that \(\sup \, B\) exists and \(\sup \, B = \sup \, A\text{.}\)
