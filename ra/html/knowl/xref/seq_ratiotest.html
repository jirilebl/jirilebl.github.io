Lemma 2.2.12. Ratio test for sequences.

Let \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) be a sequence such that \(x_n \neq 0\) for all \(n\) and such that the limit
\begin{equation} L \coloneqq \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{\sabs{x_{n+1}}}{\sabs{x_n}} \qquad \text{exists.} \end{equation}
🔗
  1. If \(L < 1\text{,}\) then \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges and \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} x_n = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If \(L > 1\text{,}\) then \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) is unbounded (hence diverges).

    🔗
