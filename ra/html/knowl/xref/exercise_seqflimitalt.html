Exercise 3.1.11.

Prove the following stronger version of one direction of Lemma 3.1.7: Let \(S \subset \R\text{,}\) \(c\) be a cluster point of \(S\text{,}\) and \(f \colon S \to \R\) be a function. Suppose that for every sequence \(\{x_n\}_{n=1}^\infty\) in \(S \setminus \{c\}\) such that \(\lim_{n\to\infty} x_n = c\) the sequence \(\bigl\{ f(x_n) \bigr\}_{n=1}^\infty\) is convergent. Then show that the limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x \to c\) exists.
