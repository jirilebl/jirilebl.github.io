Theorem 4.2.5. Cauchy’s mean value theorem.

Let \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) and \(\varphi \colon [a,b] \to \R\) be continuous functions differentiable on \((a,b)\text{.}\) Then there exists a point \(c \in (a,b)\) such that
\begin{equation} \bigl(f(b)-f(a)\bigr)\varphi'(c) = f'(c)\bigl(\varphi(b)-\varphi(a)\bigr) . \end{equation}
