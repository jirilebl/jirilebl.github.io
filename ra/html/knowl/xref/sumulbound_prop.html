Proposition 5.1.2.

Let \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) be a bounded function. Let \(m, M \in \R\) be such that for all \(x \in [a,b]\text{,}\) we have \(m \leq f(x) \leq M\text{.}\) Then for every partition \(P\) of \([a,b]\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} m(b-a) \leq L(P,f) \leq U(P,f) \leq M(b-a) .\tag{5.1} \end{equation}
