Proposition 1.5.1.

🔗
  1. Every infinite sequence of digits \(0.d_1d_2d_3\ldots\) represents a unique real number \(x \in [0,1]\text{,}\) and
    \begin{equation} D_n \leq x \leq D_n+\frac{1}{{10}^n} \qquad \text{for all } n \in \N. \end{equation}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. For every \(x \in (0,1]\) there exists an infinite sequence of digits \(0.d_1d_2d_3\ldots\) that represents \(x\text{.}\) There exists a unique representation such that
    \begin{equation} D_n < x \leq D_n+\frac{1}{{10}^n} \qquad \text{for all } n \in \N. \end{equation}

    🔗
