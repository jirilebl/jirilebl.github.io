Proposition 8.2.11.

Let \(T\) be an \(n\)-by-\(n\) invertible matrix. Then there exists a finite sequence of elementary matrices \(E_1, E_2, \ldots, E_k\) such that
\begin{equation} T = E_1 E_2 \cdots E_k , \end{equation}
and
\begin{equation} \det(T) = \det(E_1)\det(E_2)\cdots \det(E_k) . \end{equation}
