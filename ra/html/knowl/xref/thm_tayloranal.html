Theorem 11.3.6. Taylor’s theorem for real-analytic functions.

Let
\begin{equation} f(x) \coloneqq \sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k x^k \end{equation}
be a power series converging in \((-\rho,\rho)\) for some \(\rho > 0\text{.}\) Given any \(a \in (-\rho,\rho)\text{,}\) and \(x\) such that \(\sabs{x-a} < \rho-\sabs{a}\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!} {(x-a)}^{k} . \end{equation}
