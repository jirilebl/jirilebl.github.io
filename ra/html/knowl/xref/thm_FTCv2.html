Theorem 5.3.3.

Let \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) be a Riemann integrable function. Define
\begin{equation} F(x) \coloneqq \int_a^x f . \end{equation}
First, \(F\) is continuous on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Second, if \(f\) is continuous at \(c \in [a,b]\text{,}\) then \(F\) is differentiable at \(c\) and \(F'(c) = f(c)\text{.}\)
