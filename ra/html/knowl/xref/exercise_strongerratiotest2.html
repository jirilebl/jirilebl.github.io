Exercise 2.3.15.

Prove the following stronger version of Lemma 2.2.12, the ratio test. Suppose \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) is a sequence such that \(x_n \neq 0\) for all \(n\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. Prove that if
    \begin{equation} \limsup_{n \to \infty} \frac{\sabs{x_{n+1}}}{\sabs{x_n}} < 1 , \end{equation}
    then \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges to \(0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Prove that if
    \begin{equation} \liminf_{n \to \infty} \frac{\sabs{x_{n+1}}}{\sabs{x_n}} > 1 , \end{equation}
    then \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) is unbounded.
    🔗
    🔗
