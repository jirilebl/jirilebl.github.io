Example 6.2.14.

We could have used this result to define the exponential function. That is, the power series
\begin{equation} f(x) \coloneqq \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!} \end{equation}
has radius of convergence \(\rho=\infty\text{.}\) Furthermore, \(f(0) = 1\text{.}\) Differentiating term by term, we find that \(f'(x) = f(x)\text{.}\)
