Proposition 3.5.8.

Suppose \(f \colon A \to B\text{,}\) \(g \colon B \to \R\text{,}\) \(A, B \subset \R\text{,}\) \(a \in \R \cup \{ -\infty, \infty\}\) is a cluster point of \(A\text{,}\) and \(b \in \R \cup \{ -\infty, \infty\}\) is a cluster point of \(B\text{.}\) Suppose
\begin{equation} \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = b\qquad \text{and} \qquad \lim_{y \to b} g(y) = c \end{equation}
for some \(c \in \R \cup \{ -\infty, \infty \}\text{.}\) If \(b \in B\text{,}\) then suppose \(g(b) = c\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation} \lim_{x \to a} g\bigl(f(x)\bigr) = c . \end{equation}
