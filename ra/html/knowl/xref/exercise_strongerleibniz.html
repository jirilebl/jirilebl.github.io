Exercise 9.1.8.

Strengthen the Leibniz integral rule in the following way. Suppose \(f \colon (a,b) \times (c,d) \to \R\) is a bounded continuous function, such that \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) exists for all \((x,y) \in (a,b) \times (c,d)\) and is continuous and bounded. Define \(g \colon (c,d) \to \R\) by
\begin{equation} g(y) \coloneqq \int_a^b f(x,y) \,dx . \end{equation}
Then \(g\) is continuously differentiable and
\begin{equation} g'(y) = \int_a^b \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(x,y) \,dx . \end{equation}
Hint: See also Exercise 7.5.18 and Theorem 6.2.10.
