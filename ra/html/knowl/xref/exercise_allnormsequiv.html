Exercise 8.2.13.

Let \(X\) be a finite-dimensional vector space with basis \(\{ x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n \}\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. Let \(\snorm{\cdot}_X\) be a norm on \(X\text{,}\) \(c = (c_1,c_2,\ldots,c_n) \in \R^n\text{,}\) and \(\snorm{\cdot}_{\R^n}\) the standard euclidean norm on \(\R^n\text{.}\) Prove that there exist numbers \(m,M > 0\) such that for all \(c \in \R^n\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation} m \snorm{c}_{\R^n} \leq \snorm{c_1 x_1 + c_2 x_2 + \cdots + c_n x_n}_X \leq M \snorm{c}_{\R^n} . \end{equation}
    Hint: See the previous exercise.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Use part a) to show that if \(\snorm{\cdot}_1\) and \(\snorm{\cdot}_2\) are two norms on \(X\text{,}\) then there exist numbers \(m,M > 0\) (perhaps different from above) such that for all \(x \in X\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation} m \snorm{x}_1 \leq \snorm{x}_2 \leq M \snorm{x}_1 . \end{equation}
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Show that \(U \subset X\) is open in the metric defined by \(\norm{x-y}_1\) if and only if \(U\) is open in the metric defined by \(\norm{x-y}_2\text{.}\) So convergence of sequences and continuity of functions is the same in either norm.
