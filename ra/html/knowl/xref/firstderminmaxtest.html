Proposition 4.2.9.

Let \(f \colon (a,b) \to \R\) be continuous. Let \(c \in (a,b)\) and suppose \(f\) is differentiable on \((a,c)\) and \((c,b)\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. If \(f'(x) \leq 0\) whenever \(x \in (a,c)\) and \(f'(x) \geq 0\) whenever \(x \in (c,b)\text{,}\) then \(f\) has an absolute minimum at \(c\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If \(f'(x) \geq 0\) whenever \(x \in (a,c)\) and \(f'(x) \leq 0\) whenever \(x \in (c,b)\text{,}\) then \(f\) has an absolute maximum at \(c\text{.}\)

    🔗
