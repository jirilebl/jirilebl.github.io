Exercise 7.4.8.

(Challenging)   Let \(C\bigl([0,1],\R\bigr)\) be the metric space of Example 7.1.8. Let \(0\) denote the zero function. Then show that the closed ball \(C(0,1)\) is not compact (even though it is closed and bounded). Hints: Construct a sequence of distinct continuous functions \(\{ f_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) such that \(d(f_n,0) = 1\) and \(d(f_n,f_k) = 1\) for all \(n \neq k\text{.}\) Show that the set \(\{ f_n : n \in \N \} \subset C(0,1)\) is closed but not compact. See Chapter 6 for inspiration.
