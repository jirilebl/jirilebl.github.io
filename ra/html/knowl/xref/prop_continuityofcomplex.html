Proposition 11.1.3.

Suppose \(\{ z_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\text{,}\) \(\{ w_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) are sequences of complex numbers converging to \(z\) and \(w\) respectively. Then
🔗
  1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\to \infty} (z_n + w_n) = z + w\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\to \infty} z_n w_n = z w\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Assuming \(w_n \neq 0\) for all \(n\) and \(w \neq 0\text{,}\) \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\to \infty} \frac{z_n}{w_n} = \frac{z}{w}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  4. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\to \infty} \sabs{z_n} = \sabs{z}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  5. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\to \infty} \bar{z}_n = \bar{z}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
