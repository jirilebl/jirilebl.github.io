Exercise 10.1.6.

Suppose \(R \subset \R^n\) and \(S \subset \R^n\) are closed rectangles where \(R \subset S\text{.}\) Let \(h \colon S \to \R\) be a bounded function such that \(h(x) = 0\) if \(x \notin \partial R\) (the boundary of \(R\)). Show that \(h \in \sR(S)\) and
\begin{equation} \int_{S} h = 0 . \end{equation}
Hint: Write \(h\) as a sum of functions as in Exercise 10.1.4.
