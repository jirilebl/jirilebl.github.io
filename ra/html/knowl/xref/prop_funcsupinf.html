Proposition 1.3.7.

If \(f \colon D \to \R\) and \(g \colon D \to \R\) (\(D\) nonempty) are bounded
 1 
The boundedness hypothesis is for simplicity; it can be dropped if we use the extended real numbers.
functions and
\begin{equation} f(x) \leq g(x) \qquad \text{for all } x \in D, \end{equation}
then
\begin{equation} \sup_{x \in D} f(x) \leq \sup_{x \in D} g(x) \qquad \text{and} \qquad \inf_{x \in D} f(x) \leq \inf_{x \in D} g(x) .\tag{1.1} \end{equation}
