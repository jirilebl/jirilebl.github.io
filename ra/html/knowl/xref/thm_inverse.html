Theorem 8.5.1. Inverse function theorem.

Let \(U \subset \R^n\) be an open set and let \(f \colon U \to \R^n\) be a continuously differentiable function. Suppose \(p \in U\) and \(f'(p)\) is invertible (that is, \(J_f(p) \neq 0\)). Then there exist open sets \(V, W \subset \R^n\) such that \(p \in V \subset U\text{,}\) \(f(V) = W\text{,}\) and \(f|_V\) is one-to-one. Hence a function \(g \colon W \to V\) exists such that \(g(y) \coloneqq (f|_V)^{-1}(y)\text{.}\) Furthermore, \(g\) is continuously differentiable and
\begin{equation} g'(y) = {\bigl(f'(x)\bigr)}^{-1}, \qquad \text{for all } x \in V, y = f(x). \end{equation}
