Proposition 4.2.10.

🔗
  1. Suppose \(f \colon [a,b) \to \R\) is continuous, differentiable in \((a,b)\text{,}\) and \(\lim_{x \to a} f'(x) = L\text{.}\) Then \(f\) is differentiable at \(a\) and \(f'(a) = L\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Suppose \(f \colon (a,b] \to \R\) is continuous, differentiable in \((a,b)\text{,}\) and \(\lim_{x \to b} f'(x) = L\text{.}\) Then \(f\) is differentiable at \(b\) and \(f'(b) = L\text{.}\)
