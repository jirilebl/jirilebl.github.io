Exercise 3.1.9.

Let \(c_1\) be a cluster point of \(A \subset \R\) and \(c_2\) be a cluster point of \(B \subset \R\text{.}\) Suppose \(f \colon A \to B\) and \(g \colon B \to \R\) are functions such that \(f(x) \to c_2\) as \(x \to c_1\) and \(g(y) \to L\) as \(y \to c_2\text{.}\) If \(c_2 \in B\text{,}\) also suppose that \(g(c_2) = L\text{.}\) Let \(h(x) \coloneqq g\bigl(f(x)\bigr)\) and show \(h(x) \to L\) as \(x \to c_1\text{.}\) Hint: Note that \(f(x)\) could equal \(c_2\) for many \(x \in A\) (see also Exercise 3.1.14).
