Theorem 0.3.6. Principle of induction.

Let \(P(n)\) be a statement depending on a natural number \(n\text{.}\) Suppose that
🔗
  1. (basis statement) \(P(1)\) is true.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. (induction step) If \(P(n)\) is true, then \(P(n+1)\) is true.
    🔗
    🔗
Then \(P(n)\) is true for all \(n \in \N\text{.}\)
