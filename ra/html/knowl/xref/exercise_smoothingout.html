Exercise 5.3.7.

Let \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) be a continuous function. Let \(\epsilon > 0\) be a constant such that \(a+\epsilon < b-\epsilon\text{.}\) For \(x \in [a+\epsilon,b-\epsilon]\text{,}\) define
\begin{equation} g(x) \coloneqq \frac{1}{2\epsilon} \int_{x-\epsilon}^{x+\epsilon} f . \end{equation}
🔗
  1. Show that \(g\) is differentiable and find the derivative.
