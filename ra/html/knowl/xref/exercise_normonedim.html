Exercise 8.2.6.

Using the standard euclidean norm \(\R^n\text{,}\) show:
🔗
  1. Suppose \(A \in L(\R,\R^n)\) is defined for \(x \in \R\) by \(Ax \coloneqq xa\) for a vector \(a \in \R^n\text{.}\) Then the operator norm \(\snorm{A}_{L(\R,\R^n)} = \snorm{a}_{\R^n}\text{.}\) (That is, the operator norm of \(A\) is the euclidean norm of \(a\text{.}\))
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Suppose \(B \in L(\R^n,\R)\) is defined for \(x \in \R^n\) by \(Bx \coloneqq b \cdot x\) for a vector \(b \in \R^n\text{.}\) Then the operator norm \(\snorm{B}_{L(\R^n,\R)} = \snorm{b}_{\R^n}\text{.}\)

    🔗
