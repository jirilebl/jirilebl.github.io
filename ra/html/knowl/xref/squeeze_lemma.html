Lemma 2.2.1. Squeeze lemma.

Let \(\{ a_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\text{,}\) \(\{ b_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\text{,}\) and \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) be sequences such that
\begin{equation} a_n \leq x_n \leq b_n \quad \text{for all } n \in \N. \end{equation}
Suppose \(\{ a_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) and \(\{ b_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converge and
\begin{equation} \lim_{n\to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n\to \infty} b_n . \end{equation}
Then \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges and
\begin{equation} \lim_{n\to \infty} x_n = \lim_{n\to \infty} a_n = \lim_{n\to \infty} b_n . \end{equation}
