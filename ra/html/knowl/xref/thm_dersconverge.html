Theorem 6.2.10.

Let \(I\) be a bounded interval and let \(f_n \colon I \to \R\) be continuously differentiable functions. Suppose \(\{ f_n' \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges uniformly to \(g \colon I \to \R\text{,}\) and suppose \(\bigl\{ f_n(c) \bigr\}_{n=1}^\infty\) is a convergent sequence for some \(c \in I\text{.}\) Then \(\{ f_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges uniformly to a continuously differentiable function \(f \colon I \to \R\text{,}\) and \(f' = g\text{.}\)
