Definition 2.3.9.

We say \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) diverges to infinity
 2 
Sometimes it is said that \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges to infinity.
if for every \(K \in \R\text{,}\) there exists an \(M \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq M\text{,}\) we have \(x_n > K\text{.}\) In this case we write
\begin{equation} \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n \coloneqq \infty . \end{equation}
Similarly, if for every \(K \in \R\) there exists an \(M \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq M\text{,}\) we have \(x_n < K\text{,}\) we say \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) diverges to minus infinity and we write
\begin{equation} \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n \coloneqq -\infty . \end{equation}
