Definition 5.1.9.

Let \(f \colon [a,b] \to \R\) be a bounded function such that
\begin{equation} \underline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx = \overline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx . \end{equation}
Then \(f\) is said to be Riemann integrable. The set of Riemann integrable functions on \([a,b]\) is denoted by \(\sR\bigl([a,b]\bigr)\text{.}\) When \(f \in \sR\bigl([a,b]\bigr)\text{,}\) we define
\begin{equation} \int_a^b f(x)\,dx \coloneqq \underline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx = \overline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx . \end{equation}
As before, we often write
\begin{equation} \int_a^b f \coloneqq \int_a^b f(x)\,dx. \end{equation}
The number \(\int_a^b f\) is called the Riemann integral of \(f\text{,}\) or sometimes simply the integral of \(f\text{.}\)
