Modern science is described in the language of differential equations. That is, equations involving not only the unknown, but also its derivatives. The simplest nontrivial form of a differential equation is the first order ordinary differential equation
\begin{equation} y' = F(x,y) . \end{equation}
Generally, we also specify an initial condition \(y(x_0)=y_0\text{.}\) The solution of the equation is a function \(y(x)\) such that \(y(x_0)=y_0\) and \(y'(x) = F\bigl(x,y(x)\bigr)\text{.}\) See Figure 6.8 for a graphical representation of this equation called a slope field.
