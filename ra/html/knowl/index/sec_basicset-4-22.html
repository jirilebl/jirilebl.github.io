Theorem 0.3.5. DeMorgan.

Let \(A, B, C\) be sets. Then
\begin{equation} {(B \cup C)}^c = B^c \cap C^c , \qquad {(B \cap C)}^c = B^c \cup C^c , \end{equation}
or, more generally,
\begin{equation} A \setminus (B \cup C) = (A \setminus B) \cap (A \setminus C) , \qquad A \setminus (B \cap C) = (A \setminus B) \cup (A \setminus C) . \end{equation}
