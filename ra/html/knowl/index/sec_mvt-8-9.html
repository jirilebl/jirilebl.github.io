Exercise 4.2.9.

Prove the following version of L’Hôpital’s rule. Suppose \(f \colon (a,b) \to \R\) and \(g \colon (a,b) \to \R\) are differentiable functions and \(c \in (a,b)\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f(c) = 0\text{,}\) \(g(c)=0\text{,}\) \(g'(x) \neq 0\) when \(x \neq c\text{,}\) and that the limit of \(\nicefrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}\) as \(x\) goes to \(c\) exists. Show that
\begin{equation} \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} . \end{equation}
Compare to Exercise 4.1.15. Note: Before you do anything else, prove that \(g(x) \neq 0\) when \(x \neq c\text{.}\)
