Item 5.4.6.b.
Prove that the limit
\begin{equation} \gamma \coloneqq \lim_{n\to\infty} \left( \left( \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{k} \right) - \ln (n) \right) \end{equation}
exists. This constant \(\gamma\) is known as the Euler–Mascheroni constant
 2 
Named for the Swiss mathematician Leonhard Euler (1707–1783) and the Italian mathematician Lorenzo Mascheroni (1750–1800).
. It is not known if \(\gamma\) is rational or not. Approximately, \(\gamma \approx 0.5772\text{.}\)
