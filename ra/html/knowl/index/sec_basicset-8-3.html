Definition 0.3.26.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be sets. We say \(A\) and \(B\) have the same cardinality when there exists a bijection \(f \colon A \to B\text{.}\) We denote by \(\sabs{A}\) the equivalence class of all sets with the same cardinality as \(A\text{,}\) and we simply call \(\sabs{A}\) the cardinality of \(A\text{.}\)
