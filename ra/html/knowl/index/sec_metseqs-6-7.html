Exercise 7.3.7.

A set \(S \subset X\) is said to be dense in \(X\) if \(X \subset \widebar{S}\) or in other words if for every \(p \in X\text{,}\) there exists a sequence \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) in \(S\) that converges to \(p\text{.}\) Prove that \(\R^n\) contains a countable dense subset.
