As a side note, there is a famous related, and as yet unsolved, problem called the Jacobian conjecture. If \(F \colon \R^n \to \R^n\) is polynomial (each component is a polynomial) and \(J_F\) (the Jacobian determinant) is a nonzero constant, does \(F\) have a polynomial inverse? The inverse function theorem gives a local \(C^1\) inverse, but whether one can always find a global polynomial inverse is the question.
