Exercise 2.5.17.

(Challenging)   Prove Abel’s theorem:
🔗
Theorem. Suppose \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty x_n\) is a series whose sequence of partial sums is bounded, \(\{ \lambda_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) is a sequence with \(\lim_{n\to\infty} \lambda_n = 0\text{,}\) and \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \sabs{ \lambda_{n+1} - \lambda_n }\) is convergent. Then \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \lambda_n x_n\) is convergent.
