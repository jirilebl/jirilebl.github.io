Let \(U \subset \R^n\) be an open set and \(\omega\) a one-form defined on \(U\text{.}\) The integral of \(\omega\) is said to be path independent if for every pair of points \(x,y \in U\) and every pair of piecewise smooth paths \(\gamma \colon [a,b] \to U\) and \(\beta \colon [c,d] \to U\) such that \(\gamma(a) = \beta(c) = x\) and \(\gamma(b) = \beta(d) = y\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} \int_\gamma \omega = \int_\beta \omega . \end{equation}
In this case, we simply write
\begin{equation} \int_x^y \omega \coloneqq \int_\gamma \omega = \int_\beta \omega . \end{equation}
Not every one-form gives a path independent integral. Most do not.
