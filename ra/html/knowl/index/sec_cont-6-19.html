Exercise 3.2.19.

(Challenging)   A function \(f \colon I \to \R\) is convex if whenever \(a \leq x \leq b\) for \(a,x,b\) in \(I\text{,}\) we have \(f(x) \leq f(a) \frac{b-x}{b-a} + f(b) \frac{x-a}{b-a}\text{.}\) In other words, the line drawn between \(\bigl(a,f(a)\bigr)\) and \(\bigl(b,f(b)\bigr)\) is above the graph of \(f\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. Prove that if \(I = (\alpha,\beta)\) an open interval and \(f \colon I \to \R\) is convex, then \(f\) is continuous.
