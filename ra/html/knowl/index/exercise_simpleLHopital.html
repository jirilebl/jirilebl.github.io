Exercise 4.1.15.

Prove the following simple version of L’Hôpital’s rule. Suppose \(f \colon (a,b) \to \R\) and \(g \colon (a,b) \to \R\) are differentiable functions whose derivatives \(f'\) and \(g'\) are continuous functions. Suppose that at \(c \in (a,b)\text{,}\) \(f(c) = 0\text{,}\) \(g(c)=0\text{,}\) \(g'(x) \neq 0\) for all \(x \in (a,b)\text{,}\) and \(g(x) \neq 0\) whenever \(x \neq c\text{.}\) Note that the limit of \(\nicefrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}\) as \(x\) goes to \(c\) exists. Show that
\begin{equation} \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} . \end{equation}
