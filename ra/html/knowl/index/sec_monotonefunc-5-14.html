Exercise 3.6.14.

Prove that the Dirichlet function \(f \colon [0,1] \to\R\text{,}\) defined by \(f(x) \coloneqq 1\) if \(x\) is rational and \(f(x) \coloneqq 0\) otherwise, cannot be written as a difference of two increasing functions. That is, there do not exist increasing \(g\) and \(h\) such that \(f(x) = g(x) - h(x)\text{.}\)
