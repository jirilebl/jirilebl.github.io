Definition 2.5.1.

Given a sequence \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\text{,}\) we write the formal object
\begin{equation} \sum_{n=1}^\infty x_n \end{equation}
and call it a series. A series converges if the sequence \(\{ s_k \}_{k=1}^\infty\) defined by
\begin{equation} s_k \coloneqq \sum_{n=1}^k x_n = x_1 + x_2 + \cdots + x_k \end{equation}
converges. The numbers \(s_k\) are called partial sums. If the series converges, we write
\begin{equation} \sum_{n=1}^\infty x_n = \lim\limits_{k\to\infty} s_k . \end{equation}
In this case, we cheat a little and treat \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty x_n\) as a number.
🔗
If the sequence \(\{ s_k \}_{k=1}^\infty\) diverges, we say the series is divergent. In this case, \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty x_n\) is simply a formal object and not a number.
