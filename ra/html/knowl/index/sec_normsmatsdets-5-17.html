If in one basis \(A\) is the matrix representing a linear operator, then for another basis we can find a matrix \(B\) such that the matrix \(B^{-1}AB\) takes us to the first basis, applies \(A\) in the first basis, and takes us back to the basis we started with. Let \(X\) be a finite-dimensional vector space. Let \(\Phi \in L(X,\R^n)\) take a basis \(\{ x_1,\ldots,x_n \}\) to the standard basis \(\{ e_1,\ldots,e_n \}\) and let \(\Psi \in L(X,\R^n)\) take another basis \(\{ y_1, \ldots, y_n \}\) to the standard basis.
