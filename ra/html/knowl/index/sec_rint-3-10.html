Definition 5.1.3.

As the sets of lower and upper Darboux sums are bounded, we define
\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} & \underline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx \coloneqq \sup \, \bigl\{ L(P,f) : P \text{ a partition of } [a,b] \bigr\} , \\ & \overline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx \coloneqq \inf \, \bigl\{ U(P,f) : P \text{ a partition of } [a,b] \bigr\} . \end{aligned} \end{equation}
We call \(\underline{\int}\) the lower Darboux integral and \(\overline{\int}\) the upper Darboux integral. To avoid worrying about the variable of integration, we often simply write
\begin{equation} \underline{\int_a^b} f \coloneqq \underline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx \qquad \text{and} \qquad \overline{\int_a^b} f \coloneqq \overline{\int_a^b} f(x)\,dx . \end{equation}
