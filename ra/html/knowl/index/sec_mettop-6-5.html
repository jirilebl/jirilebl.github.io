Exercise 7.2.5.

Take \(\Q\) with the standard metric, \(d(x,y) = \sabs{x-y}\text{,}\) as our metric space. Prove that \(\Q\) is totally separated, that is, show that for every \(x, y \in \Q\) with \(x \neq y\text{,}\) there exist two open sets \(U\) and \(V\) such that \(x \in U\text{,}\) \(y \in V\text{,}\) \(U \cap V = \emptyset\text{,}\) and \(U \cup V = \Q\text{.}\)
