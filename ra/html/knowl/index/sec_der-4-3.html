Proposition 4.1.10. Chain rule.

Let \(I_1, I_2\) be intervals, let \(g \colon I_1 \to I_2\) be differentiable at \(c \in I_1\text{,}\) and \(f \colon I_2 \to \R\) be differentiable at \(g(c)\text{.}\) If \(h \colon I_1 \to \R\) is defined by
\begin{equation} h(x) \coloneqq (f \circ g) (x) = f\bigl(g(x)\bigr) , \end{equation}
then \(h\) is differentiable at \(c\) and
\begin{equation} h'(c) = f'\bigl(g(c)\bigr)g'(c) . \end{equation}
