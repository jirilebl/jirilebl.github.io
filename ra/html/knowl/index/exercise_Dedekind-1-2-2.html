Item 1.2.15.b.
Let \(A \subset \Q\) be a nonempty set that is bounded above such that whenever \(x \in A\) and \(t \in \Q\) with \(t < x\text{,}\) then \(t \in A\text{.}\) Further suppose \(\sup\, A \notin A\text{.}\) Show that there exists a \(y \in \R\) such that \(A = \{ x \in \Q : x < y \}\text{.}\) A set such as \(A\) is called a Dedekind cut.
