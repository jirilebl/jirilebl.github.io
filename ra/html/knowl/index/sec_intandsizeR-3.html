You have surely seen the notation for intervals before, but let us give a formal definition here. For \(a, b \in \R\) such that \(a < b\text{,}\) we define
\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} & [a,b] \coloneqq \{ x \in \R : a \leq x \leq b \}, \\ & (a,b) \coloneqq \{ x \in \R : a < x < b \}, \\ & (a,b] \coloneqq \{ x \in \R : a < x \leq b \}, \\ & [a,b) \coloneqq \{ x \in \R : a \leq x < b \} . \end{aligned} \end{equation}
The interval \([a,b]\) is called a closed interval and \((a,b)\) is called an open interval. The intervals of the form \((a,b]\) and \([a,b)\) are called half-open intervals.
