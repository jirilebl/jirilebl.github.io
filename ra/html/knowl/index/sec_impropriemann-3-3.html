Proposition 5.5.13. Integral test.

Suppose \(f \colon [k,\infty) \to \R\) is a decreasing nonnegative function where \(k \in \Z\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation} \sum_{n=k}^\infty f(n) \quad \text{converges} \qquad \text{if and only if} \qquad \int_k^\infty f \quad \text{converges}. \end{equation}
In this case
\begin{equation} \int_k^\infty f \leq \sum_{n=k}^\infty f(n) \leq f(k)+ \int_k^\infty f . \end{equation}
