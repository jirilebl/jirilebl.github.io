Proposition 5.5.5. Comparison test for improper integrals.

Let \(f \colon [a,\infty) \to \R\) and \(g \colon [a,\infty) \to \R\) be functions that are Riemann integrable on \([a,b]\) for all \(b > a\text{.}\) Suppose that for all \(x \geq a\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} \babs{f(x)} \leq g(x) . \end{equation}
🔗
  1. If \(\int_a^\infty g\) converges, then \(\int_a^\infty f\) converges, and in this case \(\abs{\int_a^\infty f} \leq \int_a^\infty g\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If \(\int_a^\infty f\) diverges, then \(\int_a^\infty g\) diverges.

    🔗
