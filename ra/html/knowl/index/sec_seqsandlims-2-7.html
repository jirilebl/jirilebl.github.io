Definition 2.1.2.

A sequence \(\{ x_n \}_{n=1}^\infty\) is said to converge to a number \(x \in \R\) if for every \(\epsilon > 0\text{,}\) there exists an \(M \in \N\) such that \(\sabs{x_n - x} < \epsilon\) for all \(n \geq M\text{.}\) The number \(x\) is called a limit of the sequence. We will prove that the limit \(x\) is unique, and so we write
 3 
In text, this may get rendered as \(\lim_{n\to\infty} x_n\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \lim_{n\to \infty} x_n \coloneqq x . \end{equation}
🔗
A sequence that converges is said to be convergent. Otherwise, we say the sequence diverges or that it is divergent.
