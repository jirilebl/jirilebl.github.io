There are different notions of summability (convergence) of a series than just the one we have seen. A common one is Cesàro summability
 3 
Named for the Italian mathematician Ernesto Cesàro (1859–1906).
. Let \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\) be a series and let \(s_n\) be the \(n\)th partial sum. The series is said to be Cesàro summable to \(a\) if
\begin{equation} a = \lim_{n\to \infty} \frac{s_1 + s_2 + \cdots + s_n}{n} . \end{equation}
