Theorem 11.7.16. Stone–Weierstrass, complex version.

Let \(X\) be a compact metric space and \(\sA\) an algebra of complex-valued continuous functions on \(X\text{,}\) such that \(\sA\) separates points, vanishes at no point, and is self-adjoint. Then the closure \(\widebar{\sA} = C(X,\C)\text{.}\)
