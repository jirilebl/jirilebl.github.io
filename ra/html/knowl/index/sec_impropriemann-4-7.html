Exercise 5.5.7.

Let \(f \colon \R \to \R\) be a Riemann integrable function on every interval \([a,b]\text{,}\) and such that \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty \babs{f(x)}\,dx < \infty\text{.}\) Show that the Fourier sine and cosine transforms exist. That is, for every \(\omega \geq 0\) the following integrals converge
\begin{equation} F^s(\omega) \coloneqq \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(t) \sin(\omega t) \,dt , \qquad F^c(\omega) \coloneqq \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(t) \cos(\omega t) \,dt . \end{equation}
Furthermore, show that \(F^s\) and \(F^c\) are bounded functions.
