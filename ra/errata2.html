Basic Analysis II: Introduction to Real Analysis, Volume II: Errata

Every book (no matter how much you paid for it) has errors and typos, especially text that is new in a given edition. I try to be as transparent as possible about any errors found, and I try to fix them as quickly as possible. Please do let me know if you find any errors or typos, so that they may be fixed. A free book relies on readers sending in corrections.

This page lists errors in the various editions. Nonmathematical typos, misspellings, and grammar or style problems are not listed here. Also not listed are things that were correct but simply deserved improvement. Of course, older editions may suffer from recently discovered errata as well.

  24. On page 135-137, the proof of Theorem 10.7.2 (change of variables) has 3 issues:
    1. In the change of variables going from \(g'(0)=I\) to a \(g'(0)=A,\) we forgot to keep track of the size of \(A^{-1} ,\) to get the estimate to work out, we need the estimate defining \(W_x\) to be \(< \frac{\epsilon}{2M}\) where \(M\) is the bound on norm of \(g'(x)^{-1} .\)

    3. The reason why the Darboux style estimate works was glossed over a bit and required the reader to note that \(\delta\) could have been picked smaller to make the partition work as a partition for Darboux.
  25. On page 146, when writing the Cauchy criterion for a series in the proof of the M-test, it should say \(m > n\) rather than \(m \geq n \) to avoid an empty sum.
  26. On page 148, on the top of the page, it should say \(a := \lim_{n\to\infty} a_n ,\) that is, the limit is in \(n\) not \(k.\)
  27. On page 159, in the proof of Corollary 11.3.7, we should also assume without loss of generality that \(b \neq 0\) as otherwise the result is trivial, but the proof does not work.
  28. On page 162, in Exercise 11.3.9, the definition of the (generalized) binomial coefficient has a typo in the numerator. It should say \(\displaystyle \binom{\alpha}{k} := \frac{\alpha(\alpha-1)\cdots(\alpha-k+1)}{k!}\).
  29. On page 165, in the proof of item (v) there is a sign error in the computation, it should say \(\displaystyle \cos(x) = \frac{e^{ix}+e^{-ix}}{2} = \frac{e^{ix}+\overline{e^{ix}}}{2} = \operatorname{Re} (e^{ix}).\)
  30. On page 166, in the proof of item (ix) we conclude \(\sin(x) \geq x\) when we should conclude \(\sin(x) \leq x\) as it states in the proposition.
  31. On page 168, there is a typo in Exercise 11.4.11 in the integral expression for the \(\arctan .\) The \(\dfrac{1}{1+t^s}\) should be \(\dfrac{1}{1+t^2}.\)
  32. On page 170, in the statement of Lemma 11.5.4, there is an unfortunate typo where the "an" in front of \(M > 0\) should be "any", as "an" implies the incorrect quantifier making the lemma useless and trivial.
  33. On page 171, in Exercise 11.5.7, the hypothesis that \(f\) is nonconstant is missing. A constant function does not have a pole at infinity.
  34. On page 178, Exercise 11.6.11 does not go through the correct proof of Peano. Picard iterates may not work, so the suggested outline is incorrect. I will replace the outline with a correct one in the next version. Thanks to Michael Anshelevich for pointing this out. Note that an example of an equation where (subsequences of) Picard iterates do not converge to a solution is given in M. Müller, Uber das Fundamentaltheorem in der Theorie der gewöhnlichen Differentialgleichungen, Math. Zeit, 26, 1927.
  35. On page 190, Exercise 11.7.8, the exercise is stated wrongly, it should say we can uniformly approximate by trigonometric polynomials continuous where \(f(-\pi)=f(\pi) .\)
  36. On page 190, Exercise 11.7.11, the hypothesis that \(X\) contains at least two points is missing.
  37. On page 194, Example 11.8.3: The general coefficient is for \(n \neq 0\) not for \(n \geq 1 .\) For both functions.
  38. On page 201, bottom of page, the series runs from \(-\infty\) not from \(\infty .\)
  39. On page 207, Exercise 11.8.10, the \(e^{-1/n}\) should be \(e^{-n} ,\) otherwise the series doesn't even converge.

May 16th 2022 edition, Version 2.6 (edition 2, 6th update):

  1. The last line of the discussion before Proposition 8.1.11 says that \(0 \in Y\) when it should say \(0 \in \operatorname{span}(Y).\)

  4. In the proof of Proposition 10.1.12, a priori the infima are taken over independent partitions, that is, infimum of \(U(P,f)-L(Q,f)\) is zero when taken over partitions \(P\) and \(Q .\) To get that infimum of \(U(P,f)-L(P,f)\) is zero, we must take a common refinement first.
  5. On page 102, in the displayed equation about halfway through, the \(R_k\) should be \(R_j .\)
  6. On page 104, in Example 10.3.6, the last displayed inequality starts with \(m^*([a,b]) \geq \) which should be removed. The inequality gives that \(m^*([a,b]) \geq b-a \) by taking the infimum.

  8. In the proof of Riemann-Lebesgue (Theorem 10.4.3), we claim that if \(R_j \subset T_\ell\), then \(M_j-m_j < 2\epsilon .\) That is actually correct but misleading, we have obtained above that \(M_j-m_j < \epsilon .\)
  9. In Exercise 10.5.3, the \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are swapped in the integral.
  10. In Example 10.6.5, it says \(D_r := B(p,r)\) is a closed disc, when it is clearly not closed. Just remove the word "closed".
  11. In Exercise 11.2.5, obviously \(X\) cannot be an arbitrary compact metric space, it was meant to be the compact interval \([a,b].\)
  12. In Proposition 11.2.7, the hypothesis that \(Y\) is complete is missing, even though it is used in the proof. (Thanks to Judah Nouriyelian)
  13. In the proof of Theorem 11.7.1, in the footnote about the delta function, the \(g(x)\) under the integral sign should be \(g(t) .\)
  14. In Exercise 11.7.10, the hypothesis that \(\mathcal{A}\) vanishes at no other point of \(X\) is missing. Thanks to Jessee Wallace for bringing this up.
  15. When talking about writing \(s_N(f;x)\) as a convolution, there is no need to change \(x-t\) to \(t-x,\) that's a typo.
  16. In Exercise 11.8.9, the boundary value should obviously be \(f(e^{i\theta}) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n e^{in\theta}.\) The \(n\) was missing in the exponent on the right hand side.

  7. In the proof of 10.7.2, throughout the proof, \((1+4\sqrt{n}\, \epsilon)\) should be \({(1+4\sqrt{n}\, \epsilon)}^n\) and in the long estimate on page 123, the three instances of \((1+4\sqrt{n}\, \epsilon)\) should be \(\frac{1}{{(1+4\sqrt{n}\, \epsilon)}^n}.\)

  11. In the caption to Figure 11.7 it says the vertical axis is from \(-e^6\) to \(e^6,\) but it should say \(-e^4\) to \(e^4.\) The approximate value \(54.6\) is correct.
