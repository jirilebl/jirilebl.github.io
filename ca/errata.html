Guide to Cultivating Complex Analysis: Errata

Every book (no matter how much you paid for it) has errors and typos, especially text that is new in a given edition. I try to be as transparent as possible about any errors found, and I try to fix them as quickly as possible. Please do let me know if you find any errors or typos, so that they may be fixed. A free book relies on readers sending in corrections.

This page lists major problems in the various editions. Simple typos, misspellings, and such are not listed here. Also not listed are things that were correct but simply deserved improvement. Of course, older editions may suffer from recently discovered errata as well.

May 20th 2025 edition (version 1.7):

  1. Page 64, towards the bottom: When showing that \(z_k \not= z_0\) the bound should be \(|g(z_k)| \geq \frac{c}{\ell 2^k} \gt 0 . \) The \(\ell\) is wrongly in the numerator.
  2. Page 68, Exercise 3.2.20: It should say \(d\bar{z} = dx - i \, dy \) and not \(d\bar{z} = dx + i \, dy . \)
  3. Page 86, Exercise 3.4.10, the definition of exponential order is off, it should say \( |f(t)| \leq M e^{ct} \) (there was a minus sign in front of the \(c,\) so what it defined was exponential decay; the exercise is possible, though unnatural, with decay, but it is trivial and the domain given is confusing).
  4. Page 110, Lemma 4.5.3: It should say \(i \dfrac{\log |\gamma(b)-p| - \log |\gamma(a)-p|}{2\pi} ,\) both in the lemma statement and in the proof. That is, the "\(-p\)" was missing.
  5. Page 111, in the proof of Lemma 4.5.5, the definition of \(\widetilde{\theta}\) is not correct, a tilde is missing on the path. That is, it should say \(\widetilde{\theta}(t) = \operatorname{Im} L_j\bigl(\widetilde{\gamma}(t)\bigr)\) and \(\widetilde{\theta}_0 = \operatorname{Im} L_1\bigl(\widetilde{\gamma}(a)\bigr).\)
  6. Page 114, beginning of section 4.6.1: It should also say \(d\bar{z} = dx - i \, dy .\)
  7. Page 149, Exercise 6.1.2, the sequence should be of bounded functions, otherwise only a tail of the sequence is necessarily uniformly bounded.
  8. Page 155, Exercise 6.2.8, this problem is simply not true as stated, try \(z^n.\) I am not sure what the original intent was, so instead of trying to fix it, it will be replaced in the next version.
  9. Page 158, Exercise 6.3.2, part c: It should say \(U = \{ z : |z| < 1 \text{ and } \operatorname{Im} z > 0 \}\) for the upper half disc.
  10. Page 172, proof of Proposition 7.2.2: when it says "\(\cos \theta\) is strictly increasing" it should obviously say "strictly decreasing". It is the negative of the cosine that is strictly increasing.
  11. Page 175, Exercise 7.2.4, the \(z_0\) should be in the boundary of \(\mathbb{D}\) not in \(\mathbb{D}\) itself.
  12. Page 177, in the proof of Theorem 7.2.5: "let \(\varphi\) achieve a maximum at \(p \in \Delta_s(p)\)" should be "let \(\varphi\) achieve a maximum at \(p \in \Delta_s(q)\)".
  13. Page 185, the inequality proving that \(-c_1 \geq 0\) has a typo it should have \(\psi\) not \(-\psi .\) However, it is perhaps slightly easier to see just from noting that if \(\psi\) has a removable singularity then \(-c_1 \log |z|\) is bounded near zero.
  14. Page 190, in the long estimate, the signs on the \(h\) in the integrals should be flipped since \(f=\varphi+h\) not \(\varphi-h .\)
  15. Page 196, the ratio at the top has an off-by-one error, it should be \(\dfrac{\prod_{n=1}^{k} (1+a_n)}{\prod_{n=1}^{k-1} (1+a_n)}=1+a_k. \)
  16. Page 197, the proof of the Cauchyness of the sum of the logarithms has a typo and only proves one direction of the required estimate (it is missing the absolute values around the \(\log |1+a_n|\)). But there is a far easier way to go about this by just noting that \(\operatorname{Log} z\) has derivative 1 at 1 and \(a_n\) goes to 0, so \(\frac{ |\operatorname{Log} (1+a_n)|}{ |a_n| }\) goes to 1, and so \(\sum |\operatorname{Log} (1+a_n)|\) converges if and only if \(\sum |a_n| \) converges.
  17. Page 197-198, in the result on reordering, the text a couple of times refers to "power series" or "series" instead of "infinite product" or "product".
  18. Page 205, top of page: A sentence motivating the general version by mistake says the exact opposite of what it should. That is, "What changes is that the zeros can no longer congregate on any point of the boundary as well as at infinity" should say "What changes is that the zeros can now congregate on any point of the boundary as well as at infinity".
  19. Page 211, Exercise 9.2.1, the set of functions \(\mathcal{F}\) should be assumed to be an algebra.
  20. Page 216, Exercise 9.3.3, the set \(W\) should be assumed to be nonempty.
  21. Page 220, statement of Schwarz reflection principle: The extended function \(F\) is, of course, valued in the complex numbers, that is, it should say "\(F \colon U \to \mathbb{C}\)".
  22. Page 228, \(f^{-1}(D)\) should be \(f^{-1}(\Delta).\)
  23. Page 230, the example universal cover of \(\mathbb{C} \setminus \{ p \}\) is not quite right, it should be \(e^z+p .\)
  24. Page 259, in the proof of Proposition A.5.5 part (ii), the \(q\) should be chosen so that \(\inf_{p \in S} d(y,p) \geq d(y,q) - \epsilon\) (note the minus). The rest of the proof is correct.
  25. Page 270, in the definition of lower Darboux integral, it should be a sup, not an inf.
  26. On page 283, proof of Theorem B.3.7 (Chain rule): The proof forgets to talk about the case where \(k=0,\) but in this case the estimate can end early since then \(\frac{\| g(q+k)-g(q) - Bk \|}{\|h\|}=0\) and so we are done.
  27. On page 283, when discussing the one-to-many dimensional map \(\varphi\) when it says "\(h\) is the dot product", it should really say that this is the scalar product with \(h.\)

  8. In Exercise 5.4.7, the existence of that \(\Gamma\) with winding number 1 is tricky, and really was not meant to be part of that exercise.   9. In the proof of Theorem 5.4.2 (Rouché), when we mention where \(\operatorname{Log}\) is defined, it says \(\mathbb{C} \setminus (\infty,0]\) when it should say \(\mathbb{C} \setminus (-\infty,0].\)

(One could argue this is not an erratum as if \(n(\Gamma;z)\) is not defined, it clearly can't be equal to 1.)
  11. In Exercise 5.4.17, there is a missing hypothesis: the limit should be nonconstant.
  12. At the end of the proof of Proposition 7.2.2, the numerator in the penultimate expression on the displayed equation is \(e^{i\delta}-e^{i\delta}\) but it should be \(e^{i\delta}-e^{-i\delta}\) (it is correct in the next expression).
  13. In the proof of Theorem 7.2.3, it says that \(M\) is the supremum of \(f\) and that should obviously be \(|f| .\)
  14. In the proof of Theorem 7.2.7, it should say that \(S\) is such that \(r < S .\)
  15. In Exercise 7.2.17, it is not strictly an erratum, but the exercise was supposed to be stated as an "if and only if" to parallel the theorem. The direction that was left out is the simple one, but doing it is good warmup for the hard part.
  16. On page 181, in the proof of Corollary 7.2.8 (the general Harnack inequality), the last inequality in the chain says \(f(\zeta) \leq 3 f(z_n)\) but it should say \(f(z_n) \leq 3 f(\xi).\)
  17. On page 181, in the proof of Corollary 7.2.8 (the general Harnack inequality), the \(C\) we found was 9 times larger than necessary. It should be \(3^{2N}\) rather than \(3^{2N+2},\) as the estimate for \(z_j\) to \(z_{j+1}\) only needs to be applied \(n-1\) times. (Strictly speaking, that's not really an erratum as it was technically correct.) It may change the answer one gives to exercise 7.2.21 if strictly following the \(C\) given in the proof.
